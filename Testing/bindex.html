What is NAS? A Simple Explanation

Network Attached Storage is like having your own private cloud—but one that lives in your office, under your control, without monthly fees.

Think of NAS as a smart external hard drive that:

  • Connects directly to your network instead of to a single computer
  • Can be accessed by everyone on your team, from anywhere
  • Automatically keeps your data safe through redundant storage
  • Runs its own operating system with helpful business applications
  • Grows easily as your storage needs increase

Frequently Asked Questions

Get quick answers to the most common questions about NAS solutions.

How much technical expertise do I need to manage a NAS?

Very little. Modern NAS systems are designed with small businesses in mind and feature intuitive setup wizards and user-friendly management interfaces. Most NAS devices can be set up in under an hour, even by someone with limited technical knowledge.

Can I access my NAS remotely when I'm not in the office?

Yes, all our NAS solutions include secure remote access capabilities that allow you and your team to access files from anywhere with an internet connection. This functionality typically works through encrypted connections and can be accessed via web browsers or dedicated mobile apps.

How does NAS protect my data from loss?

NAS systems protect your data through multiple redundancy features like RAID configurations (storing data across multiple drives), automated backup tools, snapshot technology, and encryption options. These layered protection mechanisms provide significantly better data security than standard external drives or basic cloud storage.

Will a NAS work with both Windows and Mac computers?

Yes, all our NAS systems are designed to work in mixed computing environments. They support all major operating systems including Windows, macOS, and Linux simultaneously. Files stored on the NAS can be accessed by any authorized user regardless of their operating system.
